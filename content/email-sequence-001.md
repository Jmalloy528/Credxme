## Quick Win #1: Check Your Credit (The Right Way)

Most people are either not checking their credit at all (bad) or paying for it (unnecessary).

**Here's the free, correct way:**
→ AnnualCreditReport.com (official, free, all three bureaus)
→ Credit Karma (free, updates weekly, good for monitoring)
→ Your bank app (most major banks offer free FICO scores now)

**Important:** Checking your own credit is a "soft inquiry" and NEVER hurts your score. This myth keeps people in the dark about their own financial health.

## Quick Win #2: Lower Your Utilization (Without Paying Off Debt)

Credit utilization is 30% of your score—and most people have this wrong.

If you have a $5,000 credit card and owe $2,500, you're at 50% utilization. That's hurting you.

**Two ways to fix this today:**

1. **Pay down balances** before your statement closes (not the due date—the statement close date)
2. **Request credit limit increases** on existing cards (no credit check for this usually)

Call your credit card companies today and ask for limit increases. Even $1,000 more per card drops your utilization significantly.

## Quick Win #3: Set Calendar Reminders

Here are three dates to put in your calendar RIGHT NOW:

1. **Every 4 months:** Check AnnualCreditReport.com (rotate bureaus)
2. **Every 6 months:** Use old credit cards (keep them active)
3. **12 months from now:** Review if negative items should have dropped off

Most negative items fall off after 7 years. Set a reminder to verify this happens automatically.

## The Three Credit Tiers

I categorize credit scores into three tiers:

**Subprime (300-579):** Surviving
You're paying the "poor tax" on everything. High deposits, high interest, limited options. Every financial decision is harder and more expensive.

**Fair/Good (580-699):** Getting By  
You're approved for most things, but at mediocre rates. You're not drowning, but you're not building wealth either.

**Very Good/Excellent (700-850):** Optimized
You get the best rates, the best terms, and options. Credit becomes a tool instead of a burden.

**The gap between "getting by" and "optimized" is usually just 50-100 points.**

And those points? They're fixable.
